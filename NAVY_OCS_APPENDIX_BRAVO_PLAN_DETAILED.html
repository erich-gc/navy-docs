Appendix Bravo and Sixth-Week Study Plan

13 July–12 September 2026

This version lays out the exact material to learn each day. The quoted blocks are the words to memorize. When a day says “review,” review the complete quoted material assigned on that date and all earlier material listed in the review section.

Daily Study Format

Use this format every day unless otherwise stated:

  1. Initial recall: Recite yesterday’s material without looking.
  2. Verbatim study: Memorize the quoted text assigned for the day.
  3. Inspection response: Practice beginning each answer with:
    • “Sir/Ma’am, the ___ is…”
    • “Sir/Ma’am, the ___ article of the Code of Conduct is…”
    • “Sir/Ma’am, the ___ general order of a sentry is to…”
  4. Random recall: Have someone ask for individual items out of order.
  5. Error log: Write down every omission, substitution, or incorrect word.
  6. Final repetition: Finish with one complete, spoken recitation of the day’s assigned material.

PART II — NAVY AND MARINE CORPS RANK STRUCTURE

Friday, 14 August — Navy Officer Ranks, O-1 to O-3

Memorize exactly

O-1 Ensign

Sir/Ma’am, an O-1 in the United States Navy is an Ensign. His/her insignia is a one gold bar collar device, or one 1/2-inch gold stripe outboard of one gold five-pointed star on black shoulder boards, or one 1/2-inch gold stripe below one gold five-pointed star on service dress blue uniform sleeves.

O-2 Lieutenant Junior Grade

Sir/Ma’am, an O-2 in the United States Navy is a Lieutenant Junior Grade. His/her insignia is a one silver bar collar device, or one 1/2-inch gold stripe outboard of one 1/4-inch gold stripe outboard of one gold five-pointed star on black shoulder boards, or one 1/2-inch gold stripe below one 1/4-inch gold stripe below one gold five-pointed star on service dress blue uniform sleeves.

O-3 Lieutenant

Sir/Ma’am, an O-3 in the United States Navy is a Lieutenant. His/her insignia is a two silver bar collar device, or two 1/2-inch gold stripes outboard of one gold five-pointed star on black shoulder boards, or two 1/2-inch gold stripes below one gold five-pointed star on service dress blue uniform sleeves.

Officer-Like Qualities

Judgement

The ability to weigh facts and possible solutions on which to base sound decisions.

Justice

Giving reward and punishment according to merits of the case in question; the ability to administer a system of rewards and punishments impartially and consistently.

Decisiveness

Ability to make decisions promptly and to announce them in clear, forceful manner.

Initiative

Taking action in the absence of orders.

Dependability

The certainty of proper performance of duty.

Tact

The ability to deal with others without creating offense.

Integrity

Uprightness of character and soundness of moral principles; includes the qualities of truthfulness and honesty.

Bell Procedures

Memorize exactly

Passing the word and the use of tones to announce the arrival/ departure of officers and officials are not a means of rendering honor. These are used simply to indicate the arrival and departure of Commanders, Chiefs of Staff, Chief of Staff Officers, Commanding Officers, and Civil Officials to interested personnel. Arrivals/departures are announced only during the hours between reveille and taps. (NTP 13 (B))

Bell procedure table

Rank Number of rings Sequence Example
O6 4 ding ding (pause) ding ding (pause) (appropriate position title arriving/departing/returning) Officer Training Command Newport, Arriving/Departing
O7/O8 6 ding ding (pause) ding ding (pause) ding ding (pause) (appropriate position title arriving/departing/returning) Naval Service Training Command, Arriving/Departing
O9 and above 8 ding ding (pause) ding ding (pause) ding ding (pause) ding ding (pause) (appropriate position title arriving/departing/returning) President: United States, Arriving; Chief of Naval Operations, Arriving/Departing
