Monday, July 20 – Sunday, September 13, 2026

Mission: Arrive at Navy OCS healthy, durable, and comfortably above the required PRT standards, with special emphasis on improving push-ups while also preparing for OCS-style calisthenics, running, core work, and group PT.

2. Training Priorities

  1. Push-ups 5–6 days/week
  2. Running 4 days/week
  3. OCS-style calisthenics 2 days/week
  4. Core 4–5 days/week
  5. Curl-up and dynamic core familiarity 2–3 days/week
  6. Back/posture work 3 days/week
  7. Daily mobility or stretching
  8. Avoid injury above all else

3. Why Curl-Ups Are Included

The current Navy PRT uses:

  • Push-ups
  • Forearm plank
  • 1.5-mile run

However, the OTCN pre-arrival PDF still includes curl-ups, and OCS PT may include:

  • Curl-ups
  • Crunches
  • V-ups
  • Flutter kicks
  • Bicycle kicks
  • Mason/Russian twists
  • Supermans
  • Planks
  • Push-up variations

Therefore, this plan keeps the plank as the tested core priority, but includes curl-ups and OCS-style core circuits so you are not surprised by group PT.

4. Global Rules

Push-Up Rules

  • Do not max push-ups daily.
  • Most push-up work must stop before form failure.
  • Every rep must meet Navy standard:
    • Body straight.
    • Full lockout at the top.
    • Elbows reach at least 90 degrees at the bottom.
    • Rest only in the up position.
    • No knees, thighs, hips, chest, or stomach touching the ground.

Running Rules

  • No all-out sprinting.
  • Fast running should be controlled and smooth.
  • If shin, knee, foot, or hip pain changes your stride, stop running that day.
  • Road intervals are time-based so you do not need measured distances.
  • You still need at least two measured 1.5-mile efforts before OCS.

Pain Rule

If pain is sharp, worsening, or changes how you move:

  1. Stop the workout.
  2. Replace the next run with a brisk walk.
  3. Reduce push-up volume by 50% if shoulder, elbow, or wrist pain appears.
  4. Do not “push through” injury before OCS.

12. Final Notes

Push-Up Strategy for Test Day

Use this pacing plan:

Time Action
0:00–0:30 Smooth reps, do not sprint
0:30–1:00 Short sets, brief rests in up position
1:00–1:30 Sets of 5–8 reps
1:30–2:00 Clean reps only; no sloppy grinding

Your goal is clean counted reps, not ugly reps that get rejected.

Run Strategy for 1.5 Miles

For a 12:00 1.5-mile:

Distance Split
0.5 mile 4:00
1.0 mile 8:00
1.5 mile 12:00

Do not sprint the first half-mile. Start controlled, then build.

What to Track

Record this after each workout:

  • Workout completed: yes/no
  • Push-up total
  • Run time and effort
  • Any pain
  • Sleep quality
  • Morning bodyweight if desired

On mock PRT days, record:

  • Plank time
  • Push-ups in 2 minutes
  • 1.5-mile time
  • Weather
  • Notes on pacing
